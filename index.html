Branding at Birth

How'd you get that nickname?

One day, my mother took me and my brothers to go grocery shopping. While going down the baby food aisle my brother started acting weird. All of the sudden, he began pointing up at Gerber baby jars on the shelf.

My mom took notice and encouraged him to speak up. He wasn't able to articulate what he was seeing. In frustration, he kept pointing at the shell and then back at me without an auditory response. Eventually, he squeaked out, "Geeber!" instead of "Gerber!". He mistakenly thought I was the baby featured on all the Gerber products.

From then on, I would be known as "Geeber" or "Geeb" for short. Little did I know, "Geeb" would later become my brand.

A True Digital Native

Born on-screen, bred online

  • 1987
    Geeb is Born
  • 1994
    Amateur Level Designer

    At age 7, I designed my first level for a video game using the Pie in the Sky game engine. This moment would help kickstart a short career in game design that would continued into college.

  • 1996
    Fatherly Role Model

    My dad placed in a 3D level design contest for Quake. This would further engourage me to make my own levels for Quake and other game engines. His love for technology would continue to have a big impact on me.

  • 1997
    Quake Continues

    I continued to build as new games were released. Quake 2 was the next big thing in the computer gaming world so naturally I began developing levels for it.

  • 1999
    Gearheads and Geeks

    My dad started a side business building gamming PCs for friends and family. Eventually, I took on the hobby as well. I only developed two PC from scratch, but modded them almost every year.

  • 2001
    Set Us Up the Bomb

    Counter-Strike took over the gaming world and I began making levels for the franchise.

  • 2003
    BattleGrounds Mod

    After a rough application process, I was selected to join the BattleGrounds Half-Life mod. The team comprised of developers, historians and designers from all over the world. The game was free to play and was enjoyed by thousands of fans. This experience taught me about people of different economic and cultural backgrounds, collaboration, coding and design.

  • 2005
    First Promotion — Lead Level Designer

    Acknowledging my new skillset, the BG team nominated me Lead Level Designer. By the time the final build for BG1 went public, I had made contributions to all levels in the game.

  • 2005
    Off to College

    I was accepted at Illinois State University (ISU) in a new program called, Arts Technology. My skill set would soon expand to include a wide range of digital arts.

  • 2008
    First Exhibited Artwork

    I was selected to submit work for Test Pattern Soundcheck, an Arts Technology exhibition. Imagery from my piece, "Code as a Pillar" was mentioned in local press.

  • 2009
    First Graduation

    By developing foundational skills in web development, sound engineering, video, programming, design and art installation, I became a jack of all trades in everything digital.

  • 2010
    The Recession

    Like many of my fellow graduates, I entered the professional market at the worst time in decades. This queued me to consider continuned education as a career option.

  • 2010
    Graduate Student

    After recieving an offer for a Graduate Assistantship and meeting my soon-to-be mentor Rick Valentin, I decided to stay at ISU for two more years. I focused on web development, video and digital art exhibition.

  • 2010
    A Grecian Scholar

    My research essay entitled, "Jason Salavon in Post-Pop" was selected to be presented at the 1st International Conference at ATINER. On behalf of ISU, I traveled to Athens, Greece to present my research. Years later, my paper was included in a published book entitled, Visual and Performing Arts.

  • 2010
    Freelance Developer at Shawnimals

    Luckily, my uncle was in need of a developer and I was hungry for side work. Together, we built the Ninjatown website.

  • 2011
    Web Design Chops

    At ISU's internal web agency, my mentor, Jacob DeGeal helped improve my design skills and allowed me to pitch work to clients. This would technically be my first job at an agency.

  • 2011
    Identity Reconnected

    Parterning with Philippe Moore, we put together a culminating art exhibition for our masters program. To help fund the show, we won a Friends of the Arts grant through our University and cheritable members of the local art community.

  • 2011
    Second Graduation

    I recieved my Masters of Science in Arts Technology. I would hit the job market looking for work as a creative technologist and front-end developer. I was hungry to make my mark in the industry.

  • 2011
    Front-End Developer at ARS

    My first full-time position out of school was at Aisle Rocket Studios.

  • 2012
    Lead Developer at ARS

    Back-to-front, I began to take on ARS' biggest projects. Known for being a stickler for details, I quickly positioned myself as a sudo product and project manager.

  • 2014
    Development Team Manager at ARS

    After gaining the respect of my peers and proving myself on dozens of projects, I earned a prominent leadership role in the company. My team would continue to receive compliments on our level of collaboration and execution.

  • 2016
    Development/Platform Manager at ARS

    My responsibilites as a leader expanded. In addtion to manging the development team, I was also now a Platform Manager. I was now the key stakeholder for our clients largest web platforms. From the design and code to the business KPI's, I was responsible for making experiences come to life.
