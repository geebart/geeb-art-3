Branding at Birth

How'd you get that nickname?

One day, my mother took me and my brothers to go grocery shopping. While going down the baby food aisle my brother started acting weird. All of the sudden, he began pointing up at Gerber baby jars on the shelf.

My mom took notice and encouraged him to speak up. He wasn't able to articulate what he was seeing. In frustration, he kept pointing at the shell and then back at me without an auditory response. Eventually, he squeaked out, "Geeber!" instead of "Gerber!". He mistakenly thought I was the baby featured on all the Gerber products.

From then on, I would be known as "Geeber" or "Geeb" for short. Little did I know, "Geeb" would later become my brand.

A Timeline of a Digital Life

  • 1987
    Geeb is Born
  • 1994
    My First Level Created

    At age 7, I designed my first level for a video game using the Pie in the Sky game engine.

  • 1996
    First Level for Quake

    My dad placed in a 3D level design contest for Quake and this engouraged me to make my own.

  • 1997
    First Level for Quake 2

    I continued to build as new games were released. My brothers served as my play testers.

  • 1999
    Built First PC

    My dad started a side business building gamming PCs for friends and family. Eventually, I took on the hobby as well.

  • 2001
    First Level for Counter-Strike

    My dad decided to install a LAN in our household. This meant game nights with friends and chance to play my latest creations.

  • 2003
    Level Designer at BG

    The BattleGrounds (BG) team comprised of developers from all over the world. It was an amazing experience that taught me about different cultures, collaboration, coding and design.

  • 2005
    Lead Level Designer at BG

    As I continued to perfect my skill set, the BG team nominated me Lead Level Designer. By the time the final build for BG1 went public, I had made contributions to all levels in the game.

  • 2005
    Off to College

    I was accepted at Illinois State University (ISU) in a new program called, Arts Technology.

  • 2008
    First Exhibited Artwork

    I was selected to submit work for Test Pattern Soundcheck, an Arts Technology exhibition. Imagery from my piece, "Code as a Pillar" was mentioned in local press.

  • 2009
    First Graduation

    By developing foundational skills in art installation, design, web development, sound engineering, video and programming, I became a jack of all trades in everything digital.

  • 2010
    The Great Recession

    Like many of my fellow graduates, I entered the professional market at the worst time in decades. This queued me to consider continuned education as a career option.

  • 2010
    Graduate Student at ISU

    After recieving an offer for a Graduate Assistantship and meeting my soon-to-be mentor Rick Valentin, I decided to stay at ISU for two more years. My focus would be in web development and video.

  • 2010
    A Grecian Scholar

    My research essay entitled, "Jason Salavon in Post-Pop" was selected to be presented at the 1st International Conference at ATINER. On behalf of ISU, I traveled to Athens, Greece to present my research. Years later, my paper was included in a published book entitled, Visual and Performing Arts.

  • 2010
    Freelance Developer at Shawnimals

    Luckily, my uncle was in need of a developer and I was hungry for side work. Together, we built the Ninjatown website.

  • 2011
    Web Design Intern

    At ISU's internal web agency, my mentor, Jacob DeGeal helped improve my design chops and allowed me to pitch work to clients.

  • 2011
    Identity Reconnected

    Parterning with Philippe Moore, we put together a culminating art exhibition for our masters program finale. To help fund the show, we were awarded a Friends of the Arts grant.

  • 2011
    Second Graduation

    Masters of Science in Arts Technology

  • 2011

    My first full-time position out of school was at Aisle Rocket Studios.
